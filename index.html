Visualizing Flower Varieties Spatially

The following diagrams show the color and source (i.e., seed or Nook Miles Ticket island) of each variety (genotype) of all eight flower species in Animal Crossing: New Horizons.

Most flowers have three genes, so they're represented in three dimensions as a stack of 2D planes. Roses have four genes; the fourth dimension is represented as additional stacks.

There are three possible values for each gene (for example, rr, Rr, and RR). This means each dimension is three tiles long, high, or wide.

The horizontal axis is represents the r/R gene, the slanted axis represents y/Y, and the vertical axis represents w/W. For roses, the first stack is ss, the second is Ss, and the third is SS.

Breeding

You can click a pair of varieties (or click a single variety twice) to show the offspring that can result from breeding that pair. These possible offspring will be highlighted, and the probability of getting each variety will be shown on the tile. If you click two different varieties, they will each be labeled "parent". If you click the same variety twice, it will be labeled "parents".

By experimenting with different combinations, hopefully you can get a feel for how flower breeding works. In general, when you breed varieties that are on edges or in corners, the results tend to be more limited than when you breed varieties that are more central. Breeding the centermost variety with itself can produce any other variety. Breeding a corner variety (e.g., the topmost, rightmost, frontmost variety) with itself produces only copies of that variety.

There's a slightly-expanded version of Aeter's blue rose guide in the Rose section below. You can click the highlighted text to see an illustration of each step.

Rose

  1. Breed a seed red with another seed red to get black (25% chance). You can work on this in parallel with steps 2 through 6.
    • Feed any red offspring (50% chance) back into this step if you don't have easy access to red seeds.
    • Breed the black offspring with each other to make more of the same (100% chance).
    • Discard any pink offspring.
  2. Breed a seed white with another seed white to get purple1 (25% chance). Discard any white offspring, because some of them can't breed with seed whites to make purple.
  3. Breed the purple1 with a seed yellow to get white2 (100% chance).
  4. Breed the white2 with a purple1 to get purple1 (25% chance) and purple2 (25% chance). Discard any white offspring, unless you want to figure out which ones are seed whites and which are white2.
  5. Breed each purple offspring from the previous step with a seed yellow. If the pairing ever produces a yellow2 (50% chance), you know the purple is a purple2. It might take a few tries, of course.
    • Breeding a purple1 with a seed yellow can only produce white2.
    • Breeding a purple2 with a seed yellow can produce either white2 (50% chance) and yellow2 (50% chance).
  6. Produce white3 with one or both of the following pairings:
    • Breed a purple2 with another purple2 to get white3 (25% chance). Feed any purple offspring back into Step 5.
    • Breed a yellow2 with another yellow2 to get white3 (25% chance). Discard any yellow offspring.
  7. Breed the white3 with a black to get red2 (100% chance).
    • You can breed the red2 with another red2 to get blue (1 in 64 chance), or continue breeding to make a better red hybrid.
  8. Breed the red2 with a white3 to get orange (1 in 8 chance). Most of the other offspring are useful in some way.
    • All purple offspring are purple2.
    • All yellow offspring are yellow2.
    • The miscellaneous red offspring can be bred with each other to make blue (at least 1 in 64 chance, at most 1 in 4). Just be aware that some of the red offspring resulting from these red-red pairings will never be able to make blue, so it's best to discard them or at least keep them separate from the better red varieties.
    • The white offspring are either white2 or white3, but it's not easy to tell the difference. You can cross them with seed yellow, and if you ever get any white offspring (25% chance), then both the parent and the white offspring are white2.
  9. Breed the orange with a white3 to get red3 (25% chance). The yellow2, white3, and orange offspring (25% chance each) are useful, too.
  10. Breed the red3 with another red3 to get blue (25% chance). The red3 offspring (50% chance) and white3 offspring (25% chance) are useful, too.
